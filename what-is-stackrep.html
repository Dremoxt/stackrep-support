About

What is Stackrep?

A simple system for staying active — and a free iPhone app that keeps you consistent. Here's the full picture.

The traditional approach to fitness assumes you have time. A 45-minute gym session, a proper warm-up, a cool-down, a commute. For a lot of people — parents, full-time workers, anyone with a packed schedule — that assumption doesn't hold. And when the session can't happen, nothing happens.

Stackrep is built around a different assumption: you have two minutes, multiple times a day. That's enough.

What is a micro-workout?

A micro-workout is a single set of any exercise — done whenever you have a spare moment. Between meetings. While the kettle boils. Before breakfast. After putting the kids to bed. You don't change clothes. You don't warm up. You just do the set.

Individually, each set feels trivial. Accumulated across a full day — and across weeks — it adds up to real, consistent training volume. More importantly, it builds a habit that doesn't break when your schedule changes.

Your training doesn't have to be a single, unbroken workout. Split it into small bursts throughout the day and get the same benefit.

The three principles

01

Pick a small set of exercises

Choose 6–10 exercises that cover your whole body. Chest, back, shoulders, arms, legs, core. The exact selection matters less than having one. You can always change it later.

02

Set a daily target per exercise

Decide how many sets of each exercise you want to hit in a day. Start low — 3 or 4 sets is enough. The goal is consistency, not maximum volume on day one.

03

Tap every time you complete a set

Every set gets a tap in Stackrep. That's the whole system. The app tracks your daily progress, fills your ring, and shows your consistency on a calendar over time.

Why it works

The biggest predictor of fitness progress isn't intensity — it's consistency. A moderate workout done daily beats an intense workout done occasionally every time. Micro-workouts make consistency the path of least resistance, because there's no logistical barrier to getting started.

There's also a psychological advantage: checking off a set gives you a small, immediate reward. The progress ring filling up. The calendar staying green. These small signals keep the habit alive between the days when motivation is low.

What you don't need

  • A gym membership
  • A dedicated workout hour
  • Special equipment (most exercises are bodyweight or resistance band)
  • A strict program or periodization plan
  • Rest days scheduled in advance

Who this is for

This approach was designed for people with full lives. Parents. Office workers. Anyone whose schedule is unpredictable or packed. It's not designed for competitive athletes or anyone chasing a specific performance target — for those goals, a structured program with a coach is the better path.

If your goal is to keep your body moving, maintain muscle, and feel better — without fitness becoming another thing you have to find time for — this system is for you.
